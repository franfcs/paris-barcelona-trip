🍽️ Food on a Budget & Street-Smart Tips▼

🥖 Cheap eats — the French "meal deal"

  • Boulangerie "formule" (~€6–9): baguette sandwich + drink + pastry. Look for Formule or Menu in the window.
  • Jambon-beurre (ham & butter baguette) — €4–6, the classic cheap lunch.
  • Supermarkets (Monoprix, Franprix, Carrefour City, Lidl) — sandwich + drink + fruit ≈ €6. Perfect for a Seine picnic.
  • Crêpes / kebab-grec street stands — €4–8 everywhere.
  • L'As du Fallafel (Le Marais) — famous falafel ~€9, huge.
  • Bouillon Chartier / Bouillon Pigalle — proper sit-down French, mains €9–14, no booking (just queue).

💧 Free water & money-savers

  • Ask for une carafe d'eau — restaurants must give free tap water.
  • Free public fountains everywhere, incl. sparkling Wallace & pétillante fountains — bring a refillable bottle.
  • Too Good To Go app — bakeries/restaurants sell surplus food €3–5. Works in Paris & Barcelona.
  • Eat your big meal at lunch — bistro formule midi (€12–16 for 2 courses) is far cheaper than dinner.

🍷 Drinking alcohol in public — know the difference

🇫🇷 France (Paris, Lyon, Montpellier): Generally fine — picnicking with wine by the Seine, canals & parks is normal and tolerated. A few zones/times are restricted, and being visibly drunk in public (ivresse publique) is an offence. So: a glass of wine by the river 👍, getting hammered on the street 👎.
🇪🇸 Barcelona is different: Street drinking (botellón) is banned and fined — fines can be €300+. No drinking on streets, squares or the beach. Drink in bars, restaurants, terraces or your accommodation instead. Also ignore people selling cans of beer on the street/beach — it's illegal and a common tourist trap.

🛡️ Street-smart

  • Pickpockets on the metro & at big sights (Eiffel, Sagrada, Las Ramblas) — bag zipped & in front, phone away in crowds.
  • Decline friendship bracelets / petition-signers near monuments — classic distraction scams.
  • Tap-to-pay works nearly everywhere; carry a little cash for boulangeries & markets.
