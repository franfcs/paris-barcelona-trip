Day 2 — Paris Sightseeing

Friday 4 July
Paris
Paris Plages opens
Paris’s beloved summer tradition where kilometres of the Seine riverbank are transformed into sandy beaches with palm trees, deck chairs, and free activities. This year celebrates 250 years of Franco-American friendship.
FREE

Eiffel Tower: Summit by lift
Right to the top — 276 metres up. Glass floor on the first level, Pierre Hermé macaron bar on the second, and a champagne bar at the summit serving glasses of Moët. On a clear July evening the views stretch 70 kilometres. Book timed slots well in advance.

Paris Jazz Festival
Free jazz concerts in the Parc Floral botanical garden. World-class ensembles on an open-air stage among lily ponds and towering trees. Bring a picnic blanket and wine.

Day 3 — BIRTHDAY

Sunday 5 July
Paris
Free Museum Day
July 5th is the first Sunday of the month = FREE entry to the Louvre, Musée d’Orsay, Orangerie, Musée Rodin, Musée Picasso, and more. At the Louvre: the Michelangelo & Rodin exhibition (300 works). At the Orsay: Renoir and Love — its FINAL DAY. Arrive by 9am.
FREE
Free
Carnaval Tropical de Paris
Over 4,000 costumed dancers in towering feathered headdresses samba down the Champs-Élysées at 1pm. Steel drum bands, floats, stilt-walkers. This year’s Brazilian/Amazon theme. The largest tropical carnival in Europe, around half a million spectators. Free to watch.
FREE
Free
Seine dinner cruise (birthday evening)
Float past Notre-Dame, the Louvre, and Pont Alexandre III while being served a multi-course French dinner. The highlight: the Eiffel Tower erupts into a sparkling light show every hour on the hour — 20,000 twinkling bulbs. Birthday champagne package available.
CULTURE Optional · bateauxparisiens.com
£152–280

Day 4 — Relaxed Paris Day

Monday 6 July
Paris
Sacré-Cœur & Montmartre
White-domed basilica at Paris’s highest point with panoramic views across the entire city. Cobblestoned hilltop village of winding lanes, artists’ squares, and café terraces. Where Picasso, Monet, and Renoir once lived.
Free
Canal Saint-Martin
A tree-lined waterway where Parisians gather on summer evenings with wine and cheese. Vintage shops, coffee roasters, natural wine bars. If you’ve seen Amélie, you’ll recognise it.
Free
Fondation Louis Vuitton: Calder exhibition (optional)
Frank Gehry’s glass-sailed building houses 300 works by Alexander Calder — the inventor of the mobile. Delicate sculptures that move with the air, creating an ever-shifting ballet of colour.
CULTURE

Fourvière Basilica + Roman theatre
Take the funicular up to the jaw-droppingly ornate hilltop basilica. Interior of gold mosaics, stained glass, and polished marble. Panoramic views across Lyon with the Alps on the horizon. Walk through the 2,000-year-old Roman amphitheatre ruins next door.
Free
Nuits de Fourvière: Camille Symphonique (optional)
French singer Camille performing with the full Orchestre National de Lyon at 9:30pm in the Roman amphitheatre. She uses her voice as a complete instrument — layering beatboxing, throat singing, and soaring melodies. A one-off orchestral reimagining under the stars.

Day 6 — Lyon: Pulp Night

Wednesday 8 July
Lyon
Les Halles de Lyon Paul Bocuse
Lyon’s legendary food hall named after the “Chef of the Century.” 50 vendors: regional sausages, silky Saint-Marcellin cheeses, hand-piped praline tarts, freshly shucked oysters, and the famous quenelles — pillowy pike dumplings in creamy sauce.
FOOD
£26–52
Traboules walk, Vieux Lyon
Secret passageways cutting through Renaissance buildings — push open an unmarked door, step into a shadowy passage, emerge into a sunlit courtyard with carved stone arches. Built by silk merchants in the 1500s, used by the French Resistance in WWII. Don’t miss Cour des Voraces — a breathtaking six-storey spiral staircase.
Free
PULP at Nuits de Fourvière (optional)
Jarvis Cocker and Pulp — one of the greatest British frontmen performing “Common People,” “Disco 2000,” and “Babies” in a 2,000-year-old Roman amphitheatre at 9:30pm. When the final chorus of “Common People” hits, the entire audience becomes one massive, joyous choir. Under the stars, ancient stone amplifying every note.
MUSIC nuitsdefourviere.com
£68–112

Street art walk & Jardin des Plantes
Montpellier is one of France’s best street art cities — enormous murals around Rue du Bras de Fer. Then visit the oldest botanical garden in France (1593): towering ancient trees, a lotus pond, and paths through groves that feel like a living cathedral.
Free

One of the world’s most acclaimed pianists performing at the Opera Comedie. Dazzling technical virtuosity and fearless emotional intensity. Part of a 100-event classical/jazz festival.

La Pedrera (Casa Milà)
Gaudí’s last great building. Undulating stone facade like waves of the sea. The rooftop is the highlight: surreal ventilation towers sculpted into helmeted warriors. Visit at sunset.

Museum of Contemporary Art (free Saturday 4–8pm)
Barcelona’s major contemporary art museum. 30th anniversary collection show. Free on Saturday afternoons.
Free
Flamenco at Palau de la Música Catalana (optional)
The Palau is widely considered one of the most beautiful concert halls ever built — a UNESCO World Heritage Site with an inverted stained-glass dome, mosaic flower columns, and sculptured muses. Inside, the Gran Gala Flamenco: rapid-fire guitar, guttural deep song, and fierce percussive footwork. Unforgettable.

Day 11 — Barcelona: Relaxed Day

Monday 13 July
Barcelona
Barceloneta beach
Morning swim in the Mediterranean. Chill on the sand. Lunch at a chiringuito (beach bar) with fresh grilled seafood, paella, and sangria.
Free
Gothic Quarter
Medieval labyrinth of narrow streets dating back to the Roman era. The magnificent Cathedral with 13 white geese in the cloister. Plac¸a Reial with Gaudí’s early lampposts. Hidden chapels, jazz cellars, artisan workshops.
Free
La Boqueria market
Barcelona’s most famous food market on La Rambla. Stalls piled with fresh fruit, Iberian jamon, towers of seafood, and freshly squeezed tropical juices. Been feeding Barcelona since 1217.
Free
Montjuïc Magic Fountain
Free light-and-water show on summer evenings. Enormous jets of water dance to music while rainbow lights illuminate the fountain. A quintessentially Barcelona spectacle.
FREE
Free
